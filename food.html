Food for Mental Health

When you stick to a diet of nutrient-rich foods, you’re setting yourself up for fewer mood swings and an improved ability to focus. Studies have even found that clean diets consisting of mainly whole, unprocessed foods, can help with symptoms of depression and anxiety. Eating nutritionally dense food promotes the growth of “good” bacteria, which in turn positively affects the production of dopamine and serotonin. Make sure to include these foods in your diet for better health.
